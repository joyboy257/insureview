# Option 2: Vertical Niche Focus

**Status:** Needs Decision on Which Vertical | Distribution Strategy Required

## Core Thesis

Forgettable goes broad (all Singaporeans). We focus on a specific vertical that:
1. Has acute, underserved pain
2. Forgettable won't pursue first
3. Aligns with our autopilot capabilities
4. Has accessible distribution channels

## Four Vertical Options

---

### Option A: Expatriates / Foreign Workers in Singapore

**Segment Size:** ~200K+ EP/S Pass holders + dependents

**Why Acute Pain:**
- Cross-border coverage gaps: home-country policies invalid in Singapore, Singapore policies don't cover home-country visits
- No CPF integration: can't use government health insurance framework
- Repatriation costs often not covered by employer plans
- Complex multi-jurisdiction documentation
- High churn: employment changes create coverage anxiety

**Competition:** No autopilot/fintech focused exclusively on expats

**Distribution:** Employer HR channels (high trust), expat Facebook/forums, REACH database

**Regulatory Complexity:** MEDIUM — no special MAS rules for expats, English documentation helps

**Verdict:** Most defensible niche. Cross-border complexity is genuinely hard and underserved.

---

### Option B: SMEs / Small Business Owners

**Segment Size:** ~300K businesses, 70% of workforce

**Why Acute Pain:**
- Fragmented coverage across multiple policies and insurers
- Mandatory WICA (Work Injury Compensation) compliance is complex
- Key-person insurance often overlooked
- Cyber liability awareness low but risk high
- No time for insurance admin

**Competition:** Medium — traditional brokers dominate, no autopilot player

**Distribution:** ACRA database, SMB chambers, business banks (DBS/OCBC/UOB), accounting software

**Regulatory Complexity:** MEDIUM-HIGH — WICA mandatory, business insurance less standardized

**Verdict:** Biggest absolute market, strongest B2B distribution channels.

---

### Option C: Young Families / New Parents

**Segment Size:** ~30-35K births/year, rising average first-time parent age (31-33)

**Why Acute Pain:**
- Emotional timing: parenthood triggers insurance search
- Income replacement gap: most young families underinsured
- Education planning often neglected
- Critical illness: "too young/healthy" misconception
- Policy duplication (both parents have employer + personal policies)

**Competition:** Medium — Seedly/DollarAndSense for content, traditional agents dominate

**Distribution:** Hospital labour wards (MOMS cohort), baby fairs (ParentCraft), parenting forums, baby bonus enrollment — **highly feasible**

**Regulatory Complexity:** LOW — 14-day cooling-off, standardized benefit illustrations, MAS consumer protection

**Verdict:** Easiest entry point. Clear life-event trigger, lowest regulatory barrier, feasible distribution.

---

### Option D: High-Net-Worth / Precario Singaporeans

**Segment Size:** ~150K+ HNWIs, ~1,400 family offices

**Why Acute Pain:**
- Corporate vs. personal insurance structuring (BOLI/COLI)
- Estate planning interactions with insurance
- Succession planning for business owners (key-man, buy-sell)
- Trust vs. insurance optimization
- CRS/FATCA compliance complexity

**Competition:** Low — no fintech/autopilot in this niche, private banks dominate

**Distribution:** Private banking relationships, family office networks, legal firms, accountants — **very hard to reach**

**Regulatory Complexity:** VERY HIGH — MAS + MinLaw + IRAS + ACRA all involved

**Verdict:** Highest LTV, hardest to crack. Save for later.
