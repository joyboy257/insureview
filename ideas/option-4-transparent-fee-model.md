# Option 4: Transparent Fee Model

**Status:** Needs Research | Aligned with MAS Intent | Sustainability Question

## Core Thesis

Forgettable is free now but will monetize via hidden commissions (selling policies). We charge a transparent fee from day one — like a fee-only financial advisor, but affordable and automated.

The fee-only model:
1. Removes incentive to recommend expensive policies
2. Aligns our interest with the user's interest
3. Builds trust faster than commission-based alternatives
4. MAS has been explicitly pushing fee-based advisory over commission-based for years

## The Commission Problem in Singapore

**Current State:**
- Life insurance first-year commission: **50-70% of annual premium**
- Renewal commissions: 5-15%
- This creates perverse incentive: agents recommend policies that pay them most, not best for client
- Clients don't know the commission is embedded in their premium

**What MAS Wants:**
- FAA licensing reform to distinguish "product sales" from "financial advice"
- Fee-based advisory (client pays explicit fee) vs. commission-based (seller pays)
- MAS has been pushing this for years — we align with regulatory intent

## Fee Model Options

### Option A: Per-Report Fee
- $X per deep insurance health check / gap analysis
- One-time purchase, no ongoing commitment
- **Pros:** Simple, no ongoing commitment
- **Cons:** No recurring revenue, users only pay when curious

### Option B: Monthly Subscription
- $X/month for ongoing coverage monitoring
- Includes basic check-ins, alert when life events detected
- **Pros:** Recurring revenue, aligns with ongoing value
- **Cons:** Requires continuous perceived value delivery

### Option C: Annual Membership
- $X/year for comprehensive portfolio management
- Includes quarterly reviews, scenario modeling, optimization
- **Pros:** Fanatical annual commitment, time to demonstrate value
- **Cons:** High upfront barrier for new users

### Option D: Flat Per-Policy Placement Fee
- Flat fee (e.g., $200-500) per policy recommended and placed
- Transparent: client knows exactly what we're paid
- NOT percentage of premium (keeps incentive aligned)
- **Pros:** Transparent, scalable with policy size, clear value exchange
- **Cons:** Requires purchase to happen (not just analysis)

### Option E: Hybrid Model
- Small monthly base fee ($X/month) for ongoing monitoring
- Per-policy placement fee when a purchase is made
- **Pros:** Baseline revenue + incentive-aligned transaction fee
- **Cons:** More complex to explain

## Comparable Fee Structures

### Singapore Financial Advisors
- FA firms charge 1-3% AUM (assets under management) for investment advisory
- Flat fees for comprehensive financial plans: $500-3,000
- Hourly consulting: $150-500/hour
- **No fee-only insurance advisory benchmarks found**

### Global Fee-Only Insurance Advisory
- UK IFAs (Independent Financial Advisers): some charge flat fees per advice
- US NAPFA (National Association of Personal Financial Advisors): fee-only
- **No direct insurance-specific fee benchmarks accessible**

### Forgettable's Model
- Currently **free** — backed by Antler (VC)
- Plans to monetize via policy sales (commission hidden in premium)
- **Our differentiation: transparent fee, user interest first**
